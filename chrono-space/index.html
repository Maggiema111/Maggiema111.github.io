Yingying's ChronoSpace

Unearthing Time Narratives

my ChronoSpace video

I designed this project to explore the dynamic relationship between time, nature, and the Earth’s geological history. Caves serve as natural chronicles of Earth’s past, preserving intricate records of the planet’s geological evolution. Through this
project, I aim to inspire action by showing that every layer of rock, every mineral deposit, is a testament to the timeless yet vulnerable story of our planet—a story that continues to unfold.
These underground environments have formed over millions of years, with each layer acting as a chapter in the Earth’s long narrative. By using modern data collection techniques, the project seeks to create a “Time Archive” of geological structures.
Additionally, I envision ChronoStones as a tool to address future challenges related to climate change and natural preservation by connecting geological insights with broader environmental issues. Through this project, I aim to highlight the philosophical concept of “traces of time”—the idea that each geological layer within a cave is a silent witness to the passage of time, holding the imprints of climatic shifts, natural, and the evolving state of the planet.
Point Cloud helps document complex cave structures and monitor changes over time.

Structural Analysis: 3D models from point cloud data reveal cave geometry, sediment layers, and fault lines.

Climate Records: Isotopic analysis of mapped layers provides insights into historical climate changes.

Ecosystem Mapping: Point cloud models capture spatial layouts, aiding in the preservation of unique cave ecosystems.

Climate and Erosion Monitoring: Repeated scans allow tracking of changes in mineral deposits and erosion, offering insights into environmental impacts.

Surface Modeling: After cleaning, point cloud data is converted into realistic 3D models, simulating cave lighting and textures.
Natural systems, such as climate systems and geological movements, often exhibit chaotic behavior. The formation process of caves and certain stages of Earth’s evolution can also be extremely sensitive to initial conditions. This sensitivity further increases the complexity and unpredictability of the geological evolution of caves.

Decoding the Connection

Chaos theory allows me to rethink the 3D scan model and the use of color, incorporating elements of unpredictability. Time in the cave is not a linear flow; it is stretched, compressed, and fractured, with each geological layer encapsulating a vast span of years.

This formula and the 3D model inspire me to add lighting effects that represent random fluctuations—shifting colors and shadows, silent traces left by time in the natural world.

Inspired by Lucy, I envision caves as the Earth’s “time cornerstone.” Just as Lucy explores nonlinear time, the formation of caves represents an accumulation of existence along Earth’s timeline, a physical record of natural history. Each layer and mineral deposit materializes nature’s self-recording process, connecting past events with the present.

From Caves to Interactions Among the Atmosphere, Oceans, and Biosphere

The formation of caves is closely related to dynamic changes in the atmosphere, oceans, and Earth’s crust. For example, stalagmites and stalactites in caves can reflect changes in atmospheric carbon dioxide levels, the water cycle, and water level fluctuations during glacial periods.

By combining cave data with changes in climate and ocean systems, this project simulates the interactions within the Earth system across different historical periods and predicts possible future evolution.

Future plan

UE 3d modelling Procedure
3D Laser Scanning
Use handheld 3D laser scanners to capture spatial point data on the cave surface (Quality: non-contact, high-resolution data collection).

Point Cloud Data Acquisition and Preprocessing
• Noise Removal: Eliminate noise caused by tourists, signage, and other obstructions.
• Multi-View Alignment: Align scanned data from multiple viewpoints to capture complex structures.
• Data Simplification: Simplify the data while preserving structural accuracy.

Surface Reconstruction
• Convert point cloud data into triangular meshes and parametric surfaces.

Point Cloud Modeling
• Use Autodesk ReCap to inspect, denoise, and remove unnecessary point cloud areas, then import the data into Cyclone 3DR for surface modeling.

Model Optimization
• Refine the model data in 3ds Max, addressing overlapping surfaces and gaps, and re-modeling unfinished parts.

Adding Materials and Lighting Effects
• Add materials and lighting effects to the model in 3ds Max.

Dynamic Simulation and Visualization
• Use Unreal Engine 5 to apply dynamic effects to the model, aiming for real-time visualization and to meet the needs of complex environments.
